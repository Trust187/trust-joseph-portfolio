About

Rooted in earth,
driven by heritage

Born and raised near the historic Kano city walls and gates, my early exposure to earthen monuments shaped my enduring commitment to heritage conservation. I am an architect specialising in climate-adaptive conservation and rehabilitation of earthen heritage in rapidly urbanising, climate-vulnerable contexts.

My research integrates material diagnostics, preventive conservation frameworks, and advanced digital documentation — including AI-informed analysis and digital twin technologies — to advance low-carbon and culturally grounded rehabilitation strategies across Nigeria and Africa.

Rain-induced surface erosion as a primary degradation mechanism in traditional adobe courtyard houses of Zaria, Northern Nigeria

B.Tech Undergraduate Thesis — Modibbo Adama University, Yola

Analysed rain-induced surface erosion in traditional adobe courtyard houses, examining rainfall intensity, capillary action, and material porosity. Developed climate-responsive preventive conservation recommendations for earthen heritage in semi-arid environments.
